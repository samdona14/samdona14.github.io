Mission Statement

Call for Submissions Envisioning Hell: An Art Exhibition Celebrating 701 Years of Dante’s Divine Comedy SUBMISSION DEADLINE: Monday, November 3 Exhibition Background This exhibition is a tribute to Dante’s Divine Comedy, a work that has influenced generations of writers and artists around the world. Contemporary artists will share their creative conversations with the Inferno in works of art inspired from their redesigns of Hell. Submission Requirements Art work representing your Hell A map Artist statement (1-2 pages) Submission Guidelines Artists may artistically represent their Hell in whatever medium they choose, such as a painting, comic, canto, video, model, minecraft world, photo, game, article of clothing, food item, map, etc. Each artwork needs to be accompanied by a map, which lays out all the levels of your Hell, and an artist statement, which is a one to two page explanation of your overall vision and reflections on what you learned about Dante, your world, and yourself in doing this project. Your project must include the following somewhere: between 4 and 9 levels a moral architecture, from lesser “sins” to greater “sins” fictional and real historical people evidence of contrapasso (counter-penalty or counter-suffering; poetic justice) in the punishments: a notion of suffering as something experienced by the transgressor in retribution, return, or exchange for his or her transgression. Process: Use Design Thinking to Flare and Focus your Brainstorming! Brainstorm a list of large, medium, and small “sins,” at least 10 for each category Pick your favorite 1-3 from each category and arrange them in a hierarchical order from the least to the greatest sin (remember, at least 4-9 levels total). Brainstorm 3 ironic punishments for each sin, then pick the best 1 for each sin. Make your map. The design is up to you (detail, layout, etc.), but you must label all levels (you can google “Inferno Map” images for inspiration and look at the FAQ at the bottom of this page) Brainstorm 10 ideas for an artistic representation, then pick 1 Create! Write a 1-2 page artist statement. It must contain a description of your artwork and your Hell, and it must have a reflection about the project. You can do this easily by answering the questions below then deleting the questions and integrating your answers into a connected statement. Map All levels of your Hell must be labeled by sin description or depiction of punishments is optional Your map should fit on an 8.5 x 11 piece of paper. It does not have to be fancy but does need to be clear. Artist Statement Prompts What is your artwork? Why did you choose it--what inspired you? Explain your Hell in general terms and give important details not contained in your artwork. For example, explain the moral architecture, an example or two of poetic justice, and some of the people in your Hell. What aspect of Dante are you in dialogue with? For example, how is what you’re doing related to what Dante is doing, and how are you doing something “new”? What did you learn by doing this project? About yourself? About Dante? About the concept of Hell? Frequently Asked Questions QUESTION: What happens when I turn all this in? ANSWER: With faculty help, you will put your artwork, map, and artist statement on display at school. If you have a digital piece, we will create a website that will link out to you, and we will try to find a time and place to display it. Last year, people that had digital projects put up a QR code and/or printed out a few screen shots to get the idea across, which we highly encourage. QUESTION: I think the idea of eternal punishment is too harsh. Can I make my Hell more like jail or purgatory where souls can eventually be released? ANSWER: Yes! QUESTION: Does my Hell have to be vertical like Dante’s and located under the Earth? ANSWER: No! Your Hell can be anywhere and anything--it could be a planet, a solar system, a horizontal plain, a house, a skyscraper, a city, a minecraft world, a computer game, a maze, an amusement park, whatever! QUESTION: Does my artwork have to represent my whole Hell? ANSWER: No! It can represent a small, symbolic piece or it can even just evoke the mood. It’s up to you. QUESTION: What kind of materials could I use to make my Hell? ANSWER: It’s up to you! You could use paint, pastels, pen/pencil, clay, legos, fabric, trash, recycled materials, natural materials, computers, musical instruments, furniture, toys, game boards, etc. QUESTION: Can my artwork be a map? ANSWER: Yes--but the required map and the map artwork will need to be different. In other words, the artistic version of your map should look a lot more interesting and creative than your diagram. QUESTION: Can I screw this up? ANSWER: Not if you do the work and follow the prompt. QUESTION: What if I’m a bad artist? What if I’m not creative? What if it’s lame? ANSWER: Your artistic talents are not being judged. This is about thinking up an interesting idea and communicating it in an imaginative way. It’s about having fun and expressing yourself.
